BLACKLANDSCAPESMATTER

The Black Lives Matter Movement stands for civil rights and calls upon historically silenced voices. Place gives people a sense of identity and community. Saying Black Landscapes Matter isn't arguing something like "All Lives Matter," rather I'm trying to supplement Black Lives Matter by showing places of hope, history and refuge. This site is dedicated to mapping out the history, theory, reality and people of Black Lives Matter.

This is an open source project that is usable for public use. To contribute to any of the web maps, you can find the repositories here.

This project and maps are by Madison Draper, a student in Geography @ UC Berkeley. This project is under the direction of Dr. Alicia Cowart.
